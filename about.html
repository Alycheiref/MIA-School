About MIA

Our History

The Manitoba Islamic Association (MIA) traces its roots to the late fifties and early sixties of the 20th century, when Muslim immigrants to Manitoba came together to worship, learn and socialize. Founded officially in [Year - Placeholder], MIA has grown from a small group of pioneers into a central organization for the diverse Muslim community in the province.

We have established mosques, educational institutions like the MIA Institute of Knowledge, and numerous community support programs over the years, reflecting our commitment to serving the evolving needs of Muslims in Manitoba.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To be a leading Islamic organization, recognized for excellence in service, education, and outreach, contributing positively to the multicultural fabric of Manitoba and empowering Muslims to practice their faith and achieve their full potential.

Our Mission

To foster a vibrant, inclusive, and spiritually enriched Muslim community in Manitoba, guided by the teachings of Islam. We aim to provide comprehensive religious, educational, and social services, while promoting understanding, cooperation, and positive engagement within the community and with the broader society.
